Sebokeng
History

Established in 1965, Sebokeng (meaning "gathering place" in Sesotho) was built as a residential area for Black South Africans working in the industrial "Vaal Triangle." [cite: 21, 23, 24]

Key Milestones

1965

Township Foundation

The township was founded with the initial construction of 18,772 houses to house industrial laborers working in nearby Vanderbijlpark and Vereeniging. [cite: 24, 26]

1983

Healthcare Infrastructure

Sebokeng Hospital officially opened on February 1, 1983, to serve the rapidly growing population of the region. [cite: 35]

1984

Rent Boycotts

In September 1984, Sebokeng became a flashpoint for national unrest as residents boycotted rent and service tariffs, leading to clashes that catalyzed national civil unrest. [cite: 27, 28]

1990

Sebokeng Shooting

On March 26, 1990, police opened fire on anti-apartheid protesters, an event that led to the landmark Goldstone Commission inquiry into public violence. [cite: 29, 30]

1991

Vigil Massacre

Gunmen attacked a night vigil for community leader Christopher Nangalembe on January 12, killing 39 people and nearly derailing negotiations to end apartheid. [cite: 31, 32]

2007

Economic Growth

The opening of Thabong Mall in 2007 marked a significant post-1994 commercial development for the township. [cite: 34]
