Vanderbijlpark
Legacy

Named after Dr. Hendrik van der Bijl, this "model town" was engineered for steel. Known for its unique circular layout and status as the largest steelworks hub in the Southern Hemisphere.

Historical Timeline

1942

Industrial Origin

The town was established to accommodate the expansion of ISCOR (South African Iron and Steel Industrial Corporation) after the Pretoria plant reached its capacity.

1947

Steel Ignition

The first steelworks began operating, marking the beginning of Vanderbijlpark's journey as the industrial heart of the Vaal Triangle.

1949

Official Proclamation

Vanderbijlpark was officially proclaimed as a town, designed specifically as a "model town" for steelworkers and their families.

1952

Municipal Status

The town attained municipal status following the opening of ISCOR’s second steelworks by Governor-General Dr. EG Jansen.

1966

Academic Expansion

The North-West University (NWU) Vaal Triangle Campus was established, bringing higher education to the local industrial community.
